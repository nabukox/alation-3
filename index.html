Frequently asked questions

What makes Alation's data intelligence platform different from traditional data catalogs?

Unlike traditional data catalog solutions focused on passive documentation, Alation's platform uses intelligent, agentic workflows. These workflows embed governance directly into daily data operations, automating manual tasks and aligning metadata with business outcomes.

The platform also includes a unified data products marketplace and data product builder agent to operationalize governance at scale and accelerate data-to-value workflows. These are powered by generative AI for proactive insight discovery.

How is the Agentic Data Intelligence Platform deployed and maintained in enterprise environments?

Alation's platform features a cloud-based, open, and extensible architecture that integrates seamlessly with existing data ecosystems via native connectors and APIs. Additionally, it accommodates both on-premises and multi-cloud (e.g., Azure) deployments to meet diverse enterprise needs.

By centralizing metadata, governance policies, and automation workflows, Alation's data intelligence solution ensures consistent management across all environments. In turn, this unified approach enables secure, governed access throughout distributed data infrastructures.

What existing tools and platforms does Alation integrate with?

Alation integrates with a broad range of data sources, including: Cloud data warehouses (e.g., Snowflake), Legacy systems, BI tools, Collaborative platforms like Slack and Microsoft Teams, Modern APIs

It also provides native connectors for Databricks Unity Catalog, ensuring end-to-end automation, governance, and data intelligence across diverse enterprise infrastructures.

How can businesses accelerate AI adoption while maintaining data governance and compliance?

An agentic data intelligence platform enables businesses to adopt AI quickly without sacrificing trust. By combining AI-powered automation with built-in governance policies, organizations can classify, secure, and monitor data while ensuring compliance with regulations such as GDPR, CCPA, and HIPAA. This balance helps teams scale AI initiatives with confidence, knowing that models are trained and operated on governed, high-quality data.

How do data intelligence platforms help businesses drive more confident, data-driven decisions?

Data intelligence platforms centralize metadata, governance, and lineage into a single system of record. This gives business users context about data definitions, quality, and usage, ensuring decisions are backed by trusted information. By reducing uncertainty and surfacing the "why" behind the numbers, analysts, data leaders, and data scientists in organizations can act faster and with greater confidence.

How is an agentic data intelligence platform different from traditional data catalogs?

Unlike standalone tools, an agentic data intelligence platform combines cataloging, governance, quality, lineage, and AI automation into one ecosystem. Traditional catalogs focus on discovery, observability tools monitor pipelines, and BI platforms provide reporting. An agentic platform goes further by embedding AI agents that can take action on data—such as enforcing policies, flagging quality issues, or recommending datasets—making it more proactive, integrated, and future-ready.
